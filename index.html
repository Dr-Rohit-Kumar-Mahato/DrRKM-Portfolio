About Me

I am Dr. Rohit Kumar Mahto, a researcher specializing in Plant Biotechnology, currently affiliated with the ICAR–Indian Institute of Agricultural Biotechnology (IIAB), Ranchi. My primary research interests lie in molecular breeding, legume genomics, and sustainable crop improvement, with a particular emphasis on chickpea (Cicer arietinum L.).

I obtained my Ph.D. in Plant Biotechnology from Banaras Hindu University (BHU), where my doctoral research focused on “Gene Expression Analysis in Response to VAM and Rhizobium for Identification of High-Yielding Genotypes in Chickpea.” This work aimed to elucidate key genetic responses involved in symbiotic nitrogen fixation, contributing to the broader goal of enhancing crop productivity under sustainable agricultural frameworks.

My academic trajectory includes an M.Sc. in Plant Biotechnology from BHU and a B.Sc. in Biotechnology from HNB Garhwal Central University. During my tenure as JRF/SRF at ICAR–IARI, New Delhi, and currently as a Young Professional-II at IIAB, I have been actively involved in projects concerning genome-wide association studies (GWAS), quantitative trait loci (QTL) mapping, and functional genomics related to root nodulation, biofortification, and abiotic stress tolerance.

I have co-authored over a dozen peer-reviewed articles published in internationally reputed journals, including Frontiers in Plant Science, BMC Plant Biology, and Frontiers in Genetics. My research has also led to the development and registration of SEEDFIG, a digital tool for seed shape and size analysis, under the Government of India’s copyright system.

In addition to publications, I have contributed to book chapters and presented at multiple national and international conferences on topics such as GWAS, epigenomics, and plant-microbe interactions. My research endeavors are driven by a commitment to applying integrative and systems-level approaches to crop biotechnology for addressing challenges in food security, climate resilience, and sustainable development.
